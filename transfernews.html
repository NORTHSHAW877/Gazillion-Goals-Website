TRANSFER NEWS 25/26 SEASON

Jobe Bellingham (10-June-2025)

Joins Borussia Dortmund for a record Sunderland fee; the deal is worth an initial £27.8m (€33m) and includes a 15 per cent sell-on clause.

Excluding add-ons, Bellingham will become the second most expensive signing in Borussia Dortmund's history.

Tijjani Reijnders (11-June-2025)

Manchester City signs Reijnders on a £46.5m five-year deal from AC Milan.

He scored 19 goals in two seasons.

Trent Alexander-Arnold (12-June-2025)

The English right back has left Liverpool and joined Spanish giants Real Madrid.

He departs Anfield after 354 appearances and 23 goals for the club.

Florian Wirtz (20-June-2025)

Wirtz joins Liverpool for a record £100 million, the most expensive Bundesliga transfer ever.

The 22-year-old playmaker has put pen to paper on a long-term contract with the Reds after agreeing personal terms.

Paul Pogba (28-June-2025)

Pogba signs with AS Monaco until 2027 after an 18-month doping ban.

The 32-year-old is looking to revive his career ahead of the 2026 World Cup.

Noni Madueke (18-July-2025)

The 23 year old winger joined the Gunners for 52 million pounds from Chelsea.

Madueke scored 11 goals, and provided five assists for Chelsea last season, and broke into Thomas Tuchel's England squad.

Marcus Rashford (23-July-2025)

Former Manchester United forward Marcus Rashford has joined LaLiga side FC Barcelona on loan.

The 27-year-old was no longer in favour at Old Trafford.

Rodrigo De Paul (26-July-2025)

Rodrigo De Paul has joined MLS side Inter Miami.

Paul is on loan through the 2025 Major League Soccer (MLS) season; the deal includes an option to make the transfer permanent through the 2029 MLS season.

Viktor Gyokeres (27-July-2025)

Swedish footballer and ex-Sporting Lisbon ace, Viktor Gyokeres has joined North London side Arsenal.

The Portuguese side received £55m plus £10m in add-ons, with Gyokeres joining on a five-year deal.

João Félix (29-July-2025)

The Portuguese forward left Stamford Bridge to join Al-Nassr.

Félix signed for the Saudi Pro League club for an initial fee of £26.2 million.

Luis Diaz (30-July-2025)

Bayern Munich have signed forward Luis Diaz from Premier League champions Liverpool for a reported fee that could rise to 75 million euros ($86.5 million).

The Colombian, who won the Premier League, FA Cup and League Cup during his time at Anfield, has signed a contract with the Bundesliga champions until 2029.
